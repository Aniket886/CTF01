Ancient Cryptography

Excellent work with Base64! Now let's travel back in time...

Julius Caesar used one of the earliest known ciphers to protect his military communications.

The Caesar cipher shifts each letter by a fixed number of positions in the alphabet.

Encrypted Message (ROT13):

SYNT{pnrfne_pvcure_vf_2000_lrnef_byq}

Hint: ROT13 means each letter is rotated by 13 positions

How Caesar Cipher Works:

  • A becomes B (shift of 1)
  • A becomes C (shift of 2)
  • A becomes N (shift of 13 - ROT13)
  • At the end of alphabet, it wraps around: Z becomes A (shift of 1)
